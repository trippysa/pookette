The Oxford Pooklish Dictionary™️

Your comprehensive guide to the Pooklish language

Pook
The root of all words in the Pooklish language, the foundation of communication.
Origin: from the Latin word 'pookie'
Pooketh
A male.
Pooka
A female.
Pookino
An Italian male.
Pookina
An Italian female.
Pookeaux
A French male.
Pookette
A French female.
Pooko
A white cat of enormous size and cuteness.
Pookini
Several pooks (i.e., plural).
Pooking
Used to describe what a pook would do (verb).
Example: "I am pookin around today"
Pookventure
A trip, vacation, or experience that involved adventure and fun.
Pookine
Ending of words, often signifies "pertaining to" or "like."
Example: pookine (pook-like)
Pookinator
A machine; also an adjective to describe productivity.
Tiff
Honorable mention: Colloquial slang word used to describe an argument ranging from small to large in capacity.
